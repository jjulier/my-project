Hello G.B.F Series, 2012
Thread, chrome, sponge, rollers, tape, aluminium, rubber, nylon, plastic, baby oil, bodyspray and hairspray.

In appropriating the sculpting techniques of hairdressing, extensions and highlights are added to slowly modify and enhance a sculpture's look. The process of making is rhythmic and compulsive; artworks often evolve gradually, accumulating in mass over time. The compulsive desire to reconfigure, reinvent, re-cut and re-colour derives from the satisfaction gained through succumbing to the lure of the surface. The process of overworking the sculptural surface is self-indulgent.
